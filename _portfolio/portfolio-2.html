--- title: "Micromechanics of granular materials considering their shape and deformability" excerpt: collection: portfolio --- Granular materials are among the most prevalent materials on Earth, surpassed only by water in their abundance. They exist across a vast range of scales: from micron-sized particles in clay, where chemical kinetics govern interactions, to larger assemblies like sand, gravel, and rock boulders. These coarse-grained assemblies, characterised by irregularly shaped particles, respond to mechanical probing primarily through their inter-particle contact interactions. Often times this assembly of particles is assumed as a continuum for ease of applying mathematical theories fundamentally derived from solid mechanics. Such an assumption introduces a non-physical nature into the phenomenological parameters used in constitutive models for soils. Here the multi-scale processes of granular materials are investigated to link contact-level interactions to the macro-scale behavior usind Discrete Element Method and image-aided experiments.
